01 — Overview

The problem worth solving, and who it was for.

The problem

Hiring an electrician and sourcing the right parts are two broken errands stitched together. Homeowners bounce between directories with no licensing guarantees and supply sites that assume you already know the part number. Pros, meanwhile, lose hours to unqualified leads and back-and-forth quoting.

Entheos collapses both jobs into one trusted surface: find a licensed pro, get a transparent quote, and buy the verified supplies for the job — in a single flow.

“If a homeowner can book a ride in three taps, booking a vetted electrician shouldn’t feel harder than that.”

02 — Approach

Key product and engineering decisions.

Trust as the core feature

Every professional is gated behind license verification before they can receive leads. I modeled licensing state as a first-class entity so the UI could surface a verifiable badge anywhere a pro appears — search, quote, and checkout.

One flow, two sides

The hardest design problem was serving buyers and pros from the same primitives without bloating either experience. I built a shared quote object that both parties act on, with role-aware views layered on top.

  • Sub-3-second quote request flow with smart defaults from job type
  • Geo-aware pro matching using a Maps API radius + availability
  • Stripe Connect for split payments between supplies and labor
  • Realtime quote status so neither side is left refreshing
03 — How it works

The shape of the system, at a glance.

Built on Next.js and Supabase, with Stripe Connect for split payments and a maps layer for geo-matching. The interesting part isn’t the stack, though — it’s modeling licensing, quoting, and payments as shared primitives, so a homeowner and a pro can act on the same objects without either side’s experience bloating.

The biggest bet was a single search layer spanning services and supplies: search “panel upgrade” and you get the right licensed pro and the parts for the job in one result set — not two disconnected hunts.
