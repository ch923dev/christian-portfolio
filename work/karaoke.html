Overview

The problem worth solving, and who it was for.

The problem

Karaoke night dies in the setup. Someone hunts for the video, someone else shouts out the next song, and the “queue” is a mental list that nobody agrees on. Existing apps demand sign-ups and subscriptions for what should be a five-second join.

I wanted a karaoke app with zero friction to join, a queue everyone can see, and a TV that just plays: phones become remotes, the big screen becomes the stage.

“The best party software is invisible. You open it, you sing, you never think about the app again.”

Approach

Key product and engineering decisions.

Two screens, one source of truth

The architecture splits into a controller (your phone) and a display (the TV). Both subscribe to one shared session over WebSockets, so adding a song on any phone updates the queue everywhere instantly: no refresh, no host bottleneck.

No accounts, ever

A session is a room code. Scan, name yourself, start queuing. I deliberately removed every step between “I want to sing” and singing.

  • Collaborative realtime queue synced across all devices
  • Dedicated TV display mode with a lyrics-forward layout
  • Host controls: reorder, skip, lock the queue
  • Built directly on the YouTube IFrame Player API
How it works

The shape of the system, at a glance.

Two surfaces, a phone remote and a big-screen TV, share one realtime session. The single rule that makes it work: the session is the source of truth, so a song added on any phone updates the queue everywhere at once, with no host stuck playing DJ.

The TV stays authoritative for playback while remotes only send intent: queue, skip, reorder. That one decision is what keeps a room full of phones from fighting over the big screen.
