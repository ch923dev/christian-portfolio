# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: employers and recruiters — hiring managers and senior engineers screening a backend-developer candidate. Their context: a 10–30 second first scan, usually from a LinkedIn or CV link, deciding whether this person can build production systems. Secondary: freelance clients evaluating whether to trust a project to him.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Christian De Asis. It exists to convert a scan into an interview or a contact. Positioning: **backend developer first** — payment flows, queues, webhooks, state machines, data models — with AI tooling as the acceleration multiplier, never the identity. Success = a technical reviewer leaves believing "this person makes correct systems, fast," and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Curious, rigorous, builder. Voice states facts and shows judgment; enthusiasm comes through what was built and why, not through hype adjectives. Warm enough to feel like a person, disciplined enough to feel like an engineer.

## Anti-references

- **AI-startup gradient SaaS** — glow orbs, gradient text, hype metrics, "10x" claims. (The previous version of this site leaned here; the redesign explicitly moves away.)
- **Terminal cosplay** — fake CLI windows, hacker-green mono everywhere, tech as costume.
- **Corporate CV template** — bland LinkedIn-clone resume page, generic section grid.
- **Playful/quirky portfolio** — easter eggs, cartoon illustration, novelty interactions.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over claims.** Every metric and capability traces to real build evidence (the intake/review-evidence pipeline). Unlaunched work is labeled honestly. No invented numbers.
2. **Backend owns the headline.** Systems substance (idempotency, state machines, queues, webhooks) leads every surface; AI appears once as the accelerator, in the hero subline and one workflow section.
3. **Recruiter scan-path first.** Title, strongest proof, and contact reachable in one 10-second pass. Depth is layered underneath for the reviewer who stays.
4. **Show the systems thinking.** Structure, data flow, and precision as visual motifs — earned diagrams over decoration.
5. **Rigor is the aesthetic.** Craft signals (typography, spacing, honest states, reduced-motion care) do the persuading that adjectives would otherwise fake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Keyboard operable throughout; visible focus states; `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation (already established practice in the codebase). Body text contrast ≥4.5:1 in both themes. No information carried by color alone.
